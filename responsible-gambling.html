Signs of Problem Gambling

Problem gambling can affect anyone, regardless of age, income, or background. Recognizing the warning signs early is crucial for preventing gambling from becoming a serious problem. If you or someone you know exhibits any of these behaviors, it may be time to seek help:

  • Chasing Losses: Continuously betting more money in an attempt to recover previous losses. This is one of the most dangerous patterns in problem gambling, as it often leads to escalating losses and financial hardship.
  • Betting More Than You Can Afford: Using money that was intended for bills, groceries, rent, or other essential expenses. This includes borrowing money, using credit cards, or taking out loans to fund gambling activities.
  • Gambling Affecting Your Life: When betting starts to interfere with your work, relationships, education, or other important aspects of your life. This includes missing work, neglecting family responsibilities, or isolating yourself from loved ones.
  • Lying About Gambling: Hiding the extent of your betting activities from family, friends, or loved ones. This includes lying about how much you've bet, how much you've lost, or how often you're gambling.
  • Feeling Anxious or Depressed: Experiencing anxiety, depression, or mood swings related to gambling. This includes feeling restless or irritable when you can't bet, or feeling guilty or ashamed about your gambling activities.
  • Unable to Stop or Control Betting: Making repeated unsuccessful efforts to control, cut back, or stop gambling. This includes setting limits but consistently breaking them, or promising yourself or others that you'll stop but being unable to follow through.
  • Neglecting Responsibilities: Failing to fulfill work, school, or family obligations because of gambling. This includes missing deadlines, skipping classes, or neglecting household chores or family time.
  • Borrowing Money to Gamble: Borrowing money from friends, family, or financial institutions to fund gambling activities or pay gambling debts.
  • Gambling to Escape Problems: Using gambling as a way to escape from problems, relieve feelings of helplessness, guilt, anxiety, or depression.
  • Needing to Bet More for the Same Excitement: Finding that you need to bet larger amounts of money to achieve the same level of excitement or satisfaction that you used to get from smaller bets.

If you recognize any of these signs in yourself or someone you care about, it's important to take action. Problem gambling is a treatable condition, and help is available. The sooner you seek help, the easier it is to regain control and prevent further harm.

Tools & Tips for Responsible Gambling

Responsible gambling is about maintaining control and making informed decisions. Here are practical tools and strategies that can help you gamble responsibly:

Set Limits Before You Start

Before you place any bets, establish clear limits for yourself:

  • Deposit Limits: Set a maximum amount you're willing to deposit in a day, week, or month. Most licensed sportsbooks offer tools to set these limits directly in your account settings.
  • Loss Limits: Decide in advance how much you're willing to lose, and stop betting once you reach that limit. Never chase losses by betting more than you originally planned.
  • Time Limits: Set a time limit for your betting sessions. Use a timer or alarm to remind yourself when it's time to stop, regardless of whether you're winning or losing.
  • Wager Limits: Set a maximum bet size for individual wagers. This prevents you from making impulsive large bets that could lead to significant losses.

Take Regular Breaks

Regular breaks are essential for maintaining perspective and preventing gambling from becoming compulsive:

  • Cooling-Off Periods: Take breaks of at least 24 hours between betting sessions. This gives you time to reflect on your gambling and prevents impulsive decisions.
  • Self-Exclusion Options: If you feel you need a longer break, most sportsbooks offer self-exclusion programs that allow you to temporarily or permanently restrict your access to your account.
  • Account Suspension: You can request that a sportsbook suspend your account for a specific period (often 24 hours, 7 days, 30 days, or longer). During this time, you won't be able to place bets or make deposits.

Self-Exclusion Programs

Self-exclusion is a powerful tool for individuals who need to take a break from gambling:

  • Sportsbook Self-Exclusion: Most licensed sportsbooks offer self-exclusion tools in their account settings. You can typically exclude yourself for periods ranging from 24 hours to permanently. Once self-excluded, you cannot place bets, make deposits, or access promotional offers during the exclusion period.
  • State Self-Exclusion Programs: Many states with legal sports betting offer state-wide self-exclusion programs. These programs prevent you from accessing any licensed sportsbook in your state for a specified period. Contact your state's gaming commission for more information about state self-exclusion programs.
  • Multi-State Self-Exclusion: Some programs allow you to self-exclude across multiple states, providing comprehensive protection if you travel frequently or live near state borders.

Additional Responsible Gambling Tips

  • Never Bet Under the Influence: Avoid betting when you're under the influence of alcohol or drugs, as impaired judgment can lead to poor decisions and excessive betting.
  • Don't Bet When Emotional: Avoid betting when you're feeling angry, stressed, depressed, or anxious. Emotional betting often leads to poor decisions and chasing losses.
  • Keep Track of Your Spending: Maintain a record of how much you deposit and withdraw. Review this regularly to ensure you're staying within your budget.
  • View Losses as Entertainment Costs: Treat any losses as the cost of entertainment, similar to spending money on a movie ticket or concert. Never view losses as an investment or something you need to recover.
  • Don't Use Credit Cards: Consider using only debit cards or other payment methods that limit your ability to spend money you don't have.
  • Set Aside Money for Essentials First: Before betting, ensure you've set aside money for all essential expenses, savings, and other financial obligations.

National Resources

If you or someone you know is struggling with problem gambling, help is available. These national resources provide free, confidential support 24 hours a day, 7 days a week:

National Council on Problem Gambling

Helpline: 1-800-GAMBLER (1-800-426-2537)

Available 24/7 via phone, text, or live chat. The National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG) provides free, confidential support and connects you with local resources and treatment options.

Website: https://www.ncpgambling.org/help-treatment/national-helpline-1-800-gambler/

The NCPG website offers comprehensive information about problem gambling, treatment options, support groups, and resources for family members affected by someone else's gambling problem.

Gamblers Anonymous

Gamblers Anonymous is a fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope with each other to solve their common problem and help others recover from a gambling problem.

Website: https://www.gamblersanonymous.org

Gamblers Anonymous offers free support group meetings in communities across the United States. Meetings are available in-person and online, providing a supportive environment for individuals seeking recovery from problem gambling.

Gam-Anon (For Family Members)

Gam-Anon is a support group for family members and friends affected by someone else's gambling problem. If you're struggling with how a loved one's gambling is affecting you, Gam-Anon can provide support and resources.

Website: https://www.gam-anon.org

State-Specific Help

In addition to national resources, many states with legal sports betting offer state-specific self-exclusion programs and support services. These programs allow you to restrict your access to licensed sportsbooks within your state:

  • New Jersey: The New Jersey Division of Gaming Enforcement offers a comprehensive self-exclusion program. Contact them for information about self-exclusion and state-specific resources.
  • Pennsylvania: The Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board provides self-exclusion services and resources for problem gambling support.
  • Michigan: The Michigan Gaming Control Board offers self-exclusion programs and maintains a list of approved treatment providers.
  • New York: The New York State Gaming Commission provides information about self-exclusion and problem gambling resources.
  • Ohio: The Ohio Casino Control Commission offers self-exclusion services and problem gambling support resources.
  • Illinois: The Illinois Gaming Board provides self-exclusion programs and maintains resources for problem gambling support.
  • Colorado: The Colorado Division of Gaming offers self-exclusion services and problem gambling resources.
  • Arizona: The Arizona Department of Gaming provides information about self-exclusion and problem gambling support.
  • Virginia: The Virginia Lottery, which regulates sports betting, offers self-exclusion programs and problem gambling resources.
  • North Carolina: The North Carolina State Lottery Commission provides information about self-exclusion and problem gambling support.
  • Indiana: The Indiana Gaming Commission offers self-exclusion services and problem gambling resources.
  • Tennessee: The Tennessee Education Lottery Corporation provides information about self-exclusion and problem gambling support.
  • Maryland: The Maryland Lottery and Gaming Control Agency offers self-exclusion programs and problem gambling resources.
  • Massachusetts: The Massachusetts Gaming Commission provides self-exclusion services and problem gambling support resources.
  • West Virginia: The West Virginia Lottery Commission offers self-exclusion programs and problem gambling resources.

To find information about self-exclusion in your state, visit your state's gaming commission or lottery website, or contact them directly. Most states provide online self-exclusion forms that you can complete to restrict your access to licensed sportsbooks.

Important: Self-exclusion is a serious commitment. Once you self-exclude, you cannot reverse the decision during the exclusion period. Make sure you're ready to commit to the exclusion period you choose.
